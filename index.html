About

My Intro

My name is Ryan Chen, and I welcome you to my site. I have more than 10 years of professional experience in web applications development. Building a scalable and responsive websites has always been my passion. I started my first web page when I was in high school, and it has been an amazing journey to see how the web has evolved till today.

I worked for Verizon as a Senior Software Engineer in the past 2.5 years. I was in the Verizon Lab under Internet Services Team, where we build innovate products to support company's various needs. I had an opportunity to learn and apply some of the latest technologies like AngularJS and ReactJS on the projects. It was such a rewarding experience to know that the products I worked on will be viewed and used by millions of Verizon's customers in US.

I joined Google's Brand Studio team in mid 2013 as a contract Web Developer. During the one year period I have worked with many talented developers and product managers in the team. I was assigned to the Google Social/Geo and .org group, and focused on creating and maintaining Google's various marketing sites.

Prior to Google I worked for E*TRADE Financial as a Software Engineer. I was in a mobile app team and we built a web based applications for tablets. My job was to write a clean and semantic front-end code across many different tablets devices. The app was released in March 2013, and was available to download on iTunes and Google Play at that time.

When not coding I enjoy playing tennis, listening music and watching movies. I don't travel a lot, but I have been to Japan twice and I really like its culture. I learned Japanese by myself and passed a JLPT level 3 in year 2010, but my Japanese skills didn't really go anywhere since then :P As far as the tennis goes I am a big fan of Roger Federer (hint: the greatest tennis player at all time). With his age I still believe he can win another Grand Slam title (which he did in 2017 Australian Open!). In case you are wondering, the adorable dog on top of this page is my dog, Mochi. He was a smart little thing that always brighten up my day, before he left us years ago. Really miss him!
